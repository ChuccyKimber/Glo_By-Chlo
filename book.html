Protect the glow

Aftercare, simplified

Your gem is bonded with dental-grade composite, so it's tougher than it looks — but the first 48 hours decide how long it lasts. Here's the whole protocol.

FIRST HOUR

Nothing passes the lips

No food, no drinks, no vaping, no lipstick. The bond is still finishing its cure — give it a clean, dry hour.

FIRST 48 HOURS

Baby the bond

Skip hard, sticky, and chewy foods — ice, taffy, crusty bread, gum. Brush gently and avoid alcohol-based mouthwash.

EVERY DAY AFTER

Live your life

Brush and floss normally — just go easy directly over the gem and skip gritty whitening pastes on that spot.

The do list

Brush twice a day

A clean tooth holds a bond better. Soft bristles, gentle pressure over the gem.

Floss like normal

Gems sit on the front face of the tooth — floss doesn't touch them.

Whiten first, gem second

Planning to whiten? Do it before your appointment — whitening gel can't reach under the gem.

Book removal when you're done

Professional removal is quick, painless, and leaves your enamel exactly as we found it.

The don't list

Don't pick at it

Fingernails, tongues, and curiosity are the top three gem killers. Let it live.

Don't bite the un-biteable

Pens, nails, ice, bottle caps. Your gem is bonded, not bulletproof.

Don't pull a loose gem

If it wiggles, book a removal or replacement. Pulling can take composite with it.

Don't DIY removal

Never pry a gem off at home — that's how enamel gets scratched. We remove for $20, free within 30 days.
